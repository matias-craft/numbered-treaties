About Treaty 7

Treaty 7 formed in 1877 between several First Nations and the Government of Canada. The treaty created an agreement for land sharing and future relations.

First Nations agreed to share large areas of land with settlers. In return the government promised reserve land, payments, farming tools, and support.

Key Details

  • Signed September 22, 1877.

  • Location: Blackfoot Crossing in southern Alberta.

  • Part of the numbered treaty system in Canada.

  • Created agreements between First Nations and the Canadian government.

Nations Involved

  • Siksika Nation

  • Kainai Nation

  • Piikani Nation

  • Stoney Nakoda Nations

  • Tsuut'ina Nation

These nations continue to live in Alberta today. Treaty rights remain part of Canadian law and influence government decisions.

Fun Facts

  • Treaty 7 is one of eleven numbered treaties in Canada.

  • The agreement took place near the Bow River.

  • Blackfoot Crossing is now a historic site and museum.

  • The treaty still affects land rights and government policy today.
