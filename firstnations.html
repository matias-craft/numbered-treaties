Nations Involved

Several First Nations leaders met with Canadian officials during the Treaty 7 negotiations. These nations lived across southern Alberta before the treaty signing.

Each nation held strong political systems and cultural traditions. Leaders represented their communities during negotiations.

Blackfoot Confederacy

  • Siksika Nation
  • Kainai Nation, also known as Blood Tribe
  • Piikani Nation

These nations formed the Blackfoot Confederacy. Communities relied on buffalo hunting across the plains. Leaders joined treaty discussions after major changes affected food supply and land use.

Tsuut'ina Nation

  • Located near present day Calgary
  • Participated in Treaty 7 negotiations in 1877
  • Maintains cultural traditions and governance today

Stoney Nakoda Nations

  • Bearspaw First Nation
  • Chiniki First Nation
  • Goodstoney First Nation

These nations lived near the Rocky Mountain region. Leaders joined the treaty meeting at Blackfoot Crossing and signed the agreement.

Role of Leaders

  • Leaders spoke for their communities.
  • Negotiations included discussion about land use and survival.
  • Many leaders focused on food security after buffalo decline.
  • Decisions affected future generations.

Community Facts

  • Siksika Nation reserve sits east of Calgary.
  • Kainai Nation holds one of the largest reserves in Canada.
  • Piikani Nation reserve sits near the Rocky Mountains.
  • Tsuut'ina Nation reserve borders Calgary.
  • Stoney Nakoda nations live near the foothills of Alberta.
